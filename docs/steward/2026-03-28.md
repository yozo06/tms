### Saturday Theme: Market Access & Value Chains

---

### The Problem: Smallholder Farmers Can't Prove What They Grow

Across India's Western Ghats — the very ecosystem Yogesh's Coorg land sits in — tens of thousands of smallholder agroforestry farmers cultivate diverse plots: coffee, pepper, areca, silver oak, cardamom, jackfruit. They practice what researchers now recognize as some of the world's most biodiverse shade-grown agriculture. But when they go to sell, they are invisible.

**The core problem is traceability at scale.** Larger buyers — specialty coffee exporters, certified organic aggregators, carbon credit brokers — require farm-level data: which trees are on the land, their species composition, health history, harvest records, GPS coordinates. A buyer paying ₹400/kg for specialty Robusta needs provenance. A carbon credit verifier following Verra VM0047 needs biomass estimates. An organic certifier needs three years of input logs. Without this data, small farmers can only sell into the commodity pool at ₹150/kg — the same price as someone using pesticides and monoculture.

The farmer knows their land. They walk it every day. But the knowledge lives in their head and fades with each generation. There is no farm record, no tree census, no harvest log.

**What makes this particularly painful in Coorg:** The coffee cooperatives and pepper exporters who *want* to source ethically have no scalable way to verify farm claims. They rely on self-reporting, which undermines certification credibility. The result: even farmers with pristine agroforestry practices can't access the premium market that would reward them.

### Connection to WildArc

WildArc is already building most of what's needed:

- **Arbor V1** provides the tree census: species, zones, health scores, GPS (M-02 in backlog). Every tree Yogesh catalogs is a data point that can anchor a provenance claim.
- **Activity Log** creates the audit trail certifiers need — treatment records, observations, actions taken — timestamped and linked to specific trees.
- **M-07 (Carbon Profile)** generates the biomass estimates that underpin carbon credit pre-qualification. When a Coorg farmer can show a Verra-compliant monitoring report for 200 silver oak trees, they become carbon credit eligible.
- **M-05 (CSV Export)** would let farmers hand provenance data to buyers or certifiers in a format they can ingest.

What's missing is **the aggregation layer**: right now WildArc is single-farm. The moment it becomes multi-tenant (Phase 0 completion + C-05 migration), a cooperative could onboard 50 member farms, each maintaining their own Arbor module, with the cooperative admin seeing aggregated supply data.

### Concrete Feature Suggestion

**"Harvest Lot Report" — a one-click PDF linked to a harvest entry**

When a farmer logs a harvest (M-01 yield tracking), they should be able to generate a PDF "Harvest Lot Report" that includes:
- Farm name, GPS bounding box, total land area
- Species composition (% of canopy by species)
- Health score distribution across harvested trees
- Harvest quantity, date, lot number
- QR code linking to the public farm profile
- WildArc-generated biodiversity index (M-06)

This PDF becomes the provenance document that travels with the lot to the buyer. At ₹400/kg for 500kg of specialty Coorg Robusta, a single harvest report could be worth ₹125,000 in premium pricing — many multiples of the development cost.

**Data sources already in WildArc (or in backlog):** trees table, health_observations, activity_log, yields (M-01), GPS lat/lng (M-02), Shannon H' index (M-06).
