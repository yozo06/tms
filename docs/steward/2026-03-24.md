## 3. Real-World Problem Journal

### 🌊 Tuesday Theme: Water Management & Irrigation

#### Problem: Rainwater Harvesting Timing in Western Ghats Coffee Plantations

**The Challenge:**
Coffee farmers in Coorg (Kodagu district) and the broader Western Ghats face an increasingly unpredictable monsoon. The region traditionally receives 2,500–3,500mm of annual rainfall, concentrated in June–September. However, climate variability is shifting onset dates by 2–3 weeks and creating mid-monsoon dry spells that stress coffee plants during critical fruiting stages.

The core problem: **farmers don't know when to fill their farm ponds and how to time irrigation draw-down.** Filling too early wastes stored water during a rain gap; filling too late means the pond isn't full when the October–February dry season begins. Most smallholders (2–10 acres) rely on observation and intuition, but as weather patterns shift, traditional timing cues become unreliable.

Additionally, Coorg farms often have natural water features — streams, springs, percolation areas — whose flow rates vary seasonally. Without data, farmers can't plan which water source to rely on in which month.

**Connection to WildArc:**
- **Terra module (planned):** The `water_sources` table in Terra's design spec (`docs/designs/terra/`) would track farm water features — ponds, streams, springs, wells — with capacity, GPS location, and seasonal flow data.
- **Weather integration (backlog L-01):** Open-Meteo API data combined with water source records could generate actionable alerts: "Monsoon onset predicted in 5 days — begin pond filling" or "Mid-monsoon dry spell expected — reduce irrigation draw."
- **Arbor module (active):** Tree water needs vary by species, age, and season. Correlating tree health observations with water availability data could reveal which trees are drought-stressed and need priority irrigation.

**Suggested Feature: Water Balance Dashboard**
A simple dashboard widget showing:
1. **Current water inventory** — estimated volume in each pond/tank (manual entry or sensor-based)
2. **Forecast demand** — calculated from tree count × species water needs × current weather
3. **Forecast supply** — rainfall prediction for next 7/14/30 days
4. **Recommended action** — "Irrigate Zone A (deficit: 200L)", "Defer irrigation (rain expected Thursday)"

This could start as a manual-input tool in Terra module and evolve to sensor-automated with IoT integration.

**References:**
- Haggar, J. & Schepp, K. (2012). "Coffee and Climate Change." Impacts on coffee farming in the Western Ghats documented in NatGeo and CERC reports.
- India Meteorological Department monsoon forecast data (publicly available).
- Chettimani farm's existing 1 lakh litre pond is a real-world test case for this feature.
