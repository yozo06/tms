## 3. Real-World Problem Journal

### Thursday Theme: Climate Resilience & Carbon

**Problem: The Invisible Carbon Sink — Why Farmers Can't Access Carbon Markets**

Small-scale agroforestry farmers in India — including Coorg's coffee and spice growers — sequester significant carbon through their tree canopies, yet almost none of them participate in voluntary carbon markets. The core problem is not a lack of trees; it's a lack of data. Carbon credit verification requires documented baselines: tree species, diameter at breast height, canopy coverage estimates, growth rates over time, and GPS coordinates. Most small farms have none of this.

The friction is enormous. Third-party verifiers charge ₹50,000–₹2,00,000 per audit. Standards bodies (Verra, Gold Standard) require multi-year monitoring with consistent measurement methodology. Without verified data, a farmer with 500 silver oak trees intercropped with coffee — each sequestering 10–30 kg of carbon annually — receives zero carbon income despite contributing meaningfully to regional carbon budgets.

**Connection to WildArc:** This is precisely the gap WildArc's Arbor module was built to close. WildArc already tracks species, health observations over time, tree codes, and activity logs. The missing ingredients for carbon-readiness are GPS coordinates (planned as M-02, Sprint 4) and standardized biomass estimation fields (diameter, height, canopy spread — not yet in the roadmap).

With these additions, WildArc could:
1. Generate a per-tree carbon sequestration estimate using species-specific allometric equations (published by ICFRE and FSI for Indian species)
2. Build a verified data trail automatically from normal usage — health logs become measurement events
3. Produce an export report formatted for Verra's VM0047 (Agroforestry) methodology

**Concrete Feature Suggestion:** Add a "Carbon Profile" tab to TreeDetail that shows:
- Estimated above-ground biomass based on recorded DBH + species growth coefficients
- Cumulative CO₂ equivalent sequestered since planting date
- A download button generating a monitoring report formatted for carbon credit pre-qualification

This requires only two new fields (DBH in cm, height in m) added to the tree schema — low implementation cost, potentially transformative economic impact for Yogesh's land and for other WildArc users.

**References:** ICFRE Biomass Equations for Indian Tree Species (2020); Verra VM0047 Afforestation, Reforestation, and Revegetation methodology; NABARD's agroforestry carbon pilot documentation.
